Student Life

Religious Life on Campus

Guilford has a Quaker background, and there are Quaker meetings held every Sunday. However, most students aren't Quaker.

Greek Life

Guilford has no Greek Life because of its strong Quaker ties. However, there are "theme houses," as well as interest-based clubs which provide similar opportunities.

Athletics

Guilford competes in the Old Dominion Athletic Conference, which is DIII. The most popular sports are club sports. Football and other games are attended, but generally by other sports players. However, Guilford students really love their recreational Frisbee-it is one of the quickest growing clubs on campus.

Campus Publications

The Greenleaf Review :

“Guilford’s art and literary magazine. Published by students, the magazine accepts student- and faculty-written poetry, short stories, screenplays, artwork, and photography. Published annually, the Greenleaf Review exists to encourage creative literary and artistic expression.”

The Guilfordian:

“Award-winning independent student newspaper of Guilford College and provides a lively, high quality, reliable and informative vehicle for the exchange of information, ideas, and issues. Published weekly, The Guilfordian began in 1914 and continues to provide students valuable hands-on experience in writing, reporting, editing, photography, visual design, layout, business management, sales, and advertising while earning academic credit."

Guilford Radio

WQFS, Guilfords Campus Radio Station, is ranked as the 6th best college radio station by The Princeton Review, and is popular amongst students.

Freshman Orientation

Orientation at Guilford starts in August, with one day aimed at learning to be a Guilford student. Students are introduced to the Guilford community, register for classes, get their ID photos taken, take placement tests, and register for housing.

Drug and Alcohol Safety

There's definitely a drug and alcohol culture on campus, but if a student chooses not to take part, they won't be excluded or feel pressured to join in.

Campus Safety

Students feel safe at Guilford. The campus police are present on campus, and most Guilford students say they've never felt unsafe on campus. Guilford is also located in a safer area of Greensboro. There are blue light emergency phones all over campus.

Student Body Engagement

Interests, values, and appearance of students

According to College Prowler, "Guilford is not the most attractive bunch of people." However, students at Guilford are interesting, and dress how they want to. Students are passionate about learning and having fun.

Conflict Resolution

Guilford provides the Conflict Resolution Resource Center for students and faculty as a resource to provide safe ways of working out conflicts. It provides counseling, advice, and even meditation. The center is free for all students.
